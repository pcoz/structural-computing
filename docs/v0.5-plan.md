   conjugate roots (NAE-3-like) currently fall through to the
   canonical-bases search. v0.5 derives a real T directly from the
   `(α, β)` real/imaginary parts.

## 3. Deliverable 1 — Full Cai-Lu §4 d-admissibility

### 3.1 What ships

A *sufficient* matchgate-realisability check on general (non-symmetric)
signatures at every arity. The `realisability_check` field reports
which check ran; for arity ≥ 6 odd-parity it will now be e.g.
`"plucker_arity_n_full"` (or similar) rather than `"plucker_arity_n"`
with the deferred-completeness caveat.

### 3.2 The math gap

Per `MATCHGATE_IDENTITIES.md` ("Deferred" section) and Cai-Lu 2011
§4:

- Arity 4: even+odd identities give a **sufficient** check
  (v0.4 ships this).
- Arity 5: standard Plücker enumeration is **complete** for
  even-parity by Plücker's Grassmannian result; odd-parity covered
  by the standard enumeration since `|remaining|=1` gives |S|=1
  odd identities (v0.4 ships this).
- **Arity ≥ 6 odd-parity**: the standard 4-term Plücker enumeration
  PLUS the augmented weight-1 identity (`Σ_i (-1)^i τ(e_i) τ(complement(e_i))`)
  is *tight necessary* but **not provably sufficient**. Higher
  augmented-Pfaffian Plücker relations exist:
    * **Weight-3 × weight-3 pairings** at arity 6: pair odd-weight-3
      indices that aren't bit-flip complements.
    * **Weight-1 × weight-(n-1) chained pairings** at higher arities.
    * General Cai-Lu §4 d-admissibility for d ≥ 1: the
      d-dimensional solution-subvariety framing where matchgate
      identities cut the subvariety dimension down to 0 or 1.

### 3.3 Implementation

**Engine side (`holant-tools.non_symmetric`):** new function
`matchgate_identities_arity_n_odd_augmented(tau, n)` returning the
list of higher augmented-Pfaffian Plücker polynomials beyond the
weight-1 case. Concretely:

- For arity 6: enumerate weight-3 × weight-3 pairs `(a, b)` where
  `a, b` are both bit-flip complements of weight-3 bit strings and
  `a ⊕ b ∈ {weight-2 strings}`. Each pair contributes one identity
  via the augmented Pfaffian on the 7-vertex Kasteleyn matrix.
- For general n: the d-admissibility characterisation gives the
  full set. The cleanest implementation is to compute the augmented
  Pfaffian symbolically on a generic skew matrix and read off the
  identities from the Plücker relations.

**Wrapper side (`structural_computing/compose.py`):** extend
`_check_general_realisability` to consume the new engine functions
on the odd-parity arity ≥ 6 path. The `realisability_check` field
becomes `"plucker_arity_n_full"` when ALL identities (standard +
augmented weight-1 + new higher augmented) are checked and pass.

**Tests (`tests/test_compose.py`):**
- 3-5 constructive positive cases at arity 6 odd-parity: signatures
  built from augmented-Pfaffian constructions on a generic 7-vertex
  skew matrix.
- 3-5 negative cases: perturbed versions of the above.
- 2-3 cases at arity 5 odd-parity to ensure backward compatibility.

### 3.4 Acceptance criteria

- Every constructive positive case from `MATCHGATE_IDENTITIES.md`
  and the new arity-6 corpus passes.
- Negative cases are correctly rejected.
- For arity 6 odd-parity, signatures that pass v0.4's check but
  ARE realisable AND have a distinct higher augmented identity
  validate cleanly under v0.5.
- No regression on the existing 262 tests.
- The `realisability_check` field clearly distinguishes the new
  "full" check from the v0.4 "tight necessary" check.

### 3.5 Estimated effort

**4-6 days.** The mathematics is established (Cai-Lu §4 d-admissibility);
the work is symbolic derivation of the higher-augmented identities on
the (n+1)-vertex augmented Kasteleyn matrix, then implementation +
testing. The engine functions may need to ship in holant-tools first
(a sister-repo PR) before this package can consume them.

## 4. Deliverable 2 — Lipton-Tarjan fundamental-cycle backup

### 4.1 What ships

`PlanarSeparator(auto=True)` succeeds on a broader class of planar
graphs — specifically, those with a "fat middle BFS level" where
the v0.4 simple case raises `ValueError`. The backup case uses the
spanning-tree fundamental-cycle argument from Lipton & Tarjan 1979.

### 4.2 The algorithm

The standard Lipton-Tarjan proof goes:

1. **BFS spanning tree.** Build a BFS spanning tree T_BFS of the
   input graph G. The tree partitions the non-tree edges into
   "back edges" relative to the BFS layering.
2. **Find a balanced non-tree edge.** Look for a non-tree edge
   `(u, v)` whose fundamental cycle in T_BFS — i.e., the unique
   cycle through `(u, v)` plus the tree path from `u` to `v` —
   separates the graph into two roughly-balanced halves (each
   ≤ 2n/3 vertices).
3. **Extract the separator.** The fundamental cycle's vertices (or
   a subset of them, depending on the proof variant) form the
   separator S. Size bound `|S| ≤ 2*sqrt(2n)` follows from the
   tree-height analysis of BFS spanning trees on planar graphs
   (using the planar embedding's Euler-formula constraints).

Two implementation paths:

(a) **Original Lipton-Tarjan 1979** — uses cyclic ordering of edges
    around each vertex (the rotation system) and the planar dual
    graph. Most rigorous but requires careful corner-case handling
    for non-cellular residual embeddings.

(b) **Bodlaender-style cleanup** — simpler tree-path partitioning;
    works for many practical cases but with weaker theoretical
    guarantees on the size bound.

v0.5 ships (b) first as a working backup, with the option to swap
in (a) later for adversarial inputs.

### 4.3 Implementation

`structural_computing/decompose.py`:
- New private function `_lipton_tarjan_fundamental_cycle(rotation,
  bfs_tree, levels)` returning `(S, A, B)` via the fundamental-cycle
  argument. Called by `_lipton_tarjan_separator` ONLY when the
  simple case fails.
- `_lipton_tarjan_separator` modified: on simple-case failure
  (instead of raising), call the backup; if the backup also fails,
  raise `ValueError` with a more specific message.
- Requires the rotation system as input (the simple case didn't).
  Add a clear error if `auto=True` is used on a graph dict missing
  `"rotation"`.

### 4.4 Acceptance criteria

- Adversarial planar graphs (e.g., a planar graph with one giant
  middle BFS level and small outer levels) now succeed via the
  backup.
- A documented corpus of 3-5 fat-middle-level planar graphs all
  produce valid `(S, A, B)` partitions with `|S| ≤ 2*sqrt(2n)`.
- No regression on the 11 existing Lipton-Tarjan tests.
- Disconnected and non-planar inputs still honestly raise
  `ValueError` (the backup doesn't pretend to handle these).

### 4.5 Estimated effort

**5-7 days.** The simpler Bodlaender-style backup is implementable
in a few days; the corner cases (degenerate fundamental cycles,
multi-component residuals after vertex removal) take the bulk of
the time. Skipping the full Lipton-Tarjan 1979 path keeps scope
manageable for a single release.

## 5. Deliverable 3 — Closed-form SRP for complex roots

### 5.1 What ships

`HolographicBasisPair.discover_basis` finds a basis directly via the
closed-form path for rank-2 signatures whose order-2 recurrence has
**complex conjugate roots** `r_1 = α + iβ`, `r_2 = α - iβ`. v0.4 falls
through to the canonical-bases sweep here; v0.5 catches them in O(1).

### 5.2 The math

For a real symmetric signature satisfying the recurrence
`a*z_k + b*z_{k+1} + c*z_{k+2} = 0` with **complex** roots
`r = α ± iβ`:

- The polynomial encoding is `P(u, v) = A*(r_1*u + v)^n + B*(r_2*u + v)^n`
  with `B = conjugate(A)` (since z is real).
- Decompose into real form: `A = A_r + i*A_i`, `r_1*u + v = (αu + v) + iβu`.
  Then `A*(r_1*u + v)^n + conj * conj = 2*Re[A*((αu+v) + iβu)^n]`.
- This polynomial is real and quadratic-in-(α, β) on each `(u, v)`
  monomial.
- A real basis `T` that brings the signature to matchgate-standard
  form is `T = [[1, -α], [0, β]]` (or its row-permuted variant) —
  this sends the real and imaginary axes of `r_1*u + v` to pure
  axes `(αu+v) → u'` and `iβu → v'`, modulo normalisation.

### 5.3 Implementation

`structural_computing/compose.py`:
- `_basis_from_recurrence_kernel` extended: when `disc < 0` (complex
  roots), compute `α = -b/(2c)` and `β = sqrt(-disc)/(2c)`. Return
  `T = [[1, -α], [0, β]]` (or `T = [[1, 0], [-α, β]]` depending
  on the polynomial-encoding convention -- to be verified
  empirically on NAE-3 as the canonical test case).
- The conjugate-pair case is the only new branch; the existing
  real-root and degenerate paths remain unchanged.

### 5.4 Acceptance criteria

- NAE-3 `[0, 1, 1, 0]` finds its basis via the new closed-form
  path BEFORE the canonical-bases sweep. The discovered T should
  match (or be equivalent up to scale to) Hadamard.
- A documented corpus of 5-10 complex-roots signatures (varying
  α, β) all find bases.
- 100 random complex-roots-derived signatures (α, β sampled
  from `[-10, +10]`) all find bases.
- No regression on existing real-roots SRP tests.

### 5.5 Estimated effort

**2-3 days.** The math is straightforward; the convention-checking
(MUST match compose.py's substitution convention) is the bulk of
the time. Verifying on NAE-3 + a parametric corpus closes it out.

## 6. Sequencing

The three deliverables are **independent** and can run in parallel,
but a single-stream implementation should order them by risk:

```
SRP complex-roots (2-3 days)
  ↓
Lipton-Tarjan backup (5-7 days)
  ↓
d-admissibility (4-6 days, possibly + holant-tools PR)
```

**Rationale:**

1. **SRP first** — lowest risk, builds confidence with the
   convention-checking that the v0.4 work surfaced as a foot-gun.
2. **Lipton-Tarjan second** — moderate effort, algorithmically self-
   contained, doesn't depend on cross-repo work.
3. **d-admissibility last** — highest math content, may require
   shipping new functions in `holant-tools` first. Park-and-iterate
   if the math turns out to need a deeper Cai-Lu §4 deep-dive.

Total elapsed: **~2 weeks** single-stream; **~1 week** with two-stream
parallelism (SRP + Lipton-Tarjan in parallel, then d-admissibility).
